## 1. Overview

This document describes how to convert a **Creality CR‑Touch** auto‑leveling sensor into a **compact Z‑probe** suitable for macro‑scale SPM (Scanning Probe Microscopy) experiments.

The CR‑Touch is ideal because it already contains:

- A **spring‑loaded probe pin**
- A **tiny internal magnet**
- A **Hall‑effect position sensor**
- A **microcontroller** that outputs a clean digital trigger
- A **compact housing** (< 30 mm total length)

By replacing the stock probe pin with a **sharp pogo‑pin**, the CR‑Touch becomes a precise contact‑probe with sub‑0.1 mm Z‑resolution.

---

## 2. Internal Working Principle

The CR‑Touch is **not** inductive or optical.  
It is a **Hall‑effect displacement sensor**:

- The probe pin contains a small magnet.
- As the pin moves, the magnet shifts relative to a Hall‑sensor.
- The microcontroller detects the exact moment the pin reaches a calibrated position.
- A digital signal is output (HIGH/LOW).

This is functionally similar to a miniature Z‑force probe.

## 4. Mechanical Modification Procedure

### Step 1 — Remove the original probe pin
1. Unscrew the bottom cap of the CR‑Touch.
2. Pull out the stock 2.5 mm probe pin.
3. Keep the original spring.

### Step 2 — Insert the pogo pin
1. Insert **P100‑B1** or **P75‑B1** into the same channel.
2. Ensure the pin slides smoothly.
3. Reuse the original spring.

### Step 3 — Add magnet (only if needed)
If the pogo pin does not contain a magnet:

1. Glue a **2×2 mm neodymium magnet** to the top of the pogo pin.
2. Ensure the magnet is centered.
3. Verify free movement.

### Step 4 — Reassemble the housing
1. Reinstall the bottom cap.
2. Verify the pin moves 3–4 mm freely.

### Step 5 — Optional: Disable solenoid
The solenoid is not required for SPM use.

You may:
- Leave it connected (harmless), or
- Disconnect the solenoid wires (Hall sensor still works)

---

## 5. Electrical Interface

The CR‑Touch outputs a **digital trigger signal**:

- **LOW** → probe up  
- **HIGH** → probe triggered (contact detected)

This can be connected to:

- A microcontroller (Arduino, ESP32, STM32)
- The Prusa MK4 Z‑probe input
- A custom SPM controller

Power requirements:
- 5 V supply
- GND
- Signal output

---

## 6. Using the CR‑Touch as an SPM Z‑Probe

### Basic scanning loop:

1. Move to XY coordinate.
2. Slowly lower Z.
3. When the probe tip touches the surface → CR‑Touch triggers.
4. Record Z height.
5. Retract Z slightly.
6. Move to next XY point.
7. Repeat.

This produces a **contact‑mode height map** (macro‑SPM).

## 7. Performance Notes

- **Resolution:** ~0.01–0.05 mm (depends on mechanics)
- **Repeatability:** excellent for macro‑SPM
- **Total size:** < 30 mm
- **Tip diameter:** 1.0–1.5 mm (P75/P100)
- **Tip sharpness:** can be upgraded with tungsten tip

This is not AFM or STM, but ideal for:
- Macro‑surface mapping
- Force‑triggered scanning
- Roughness measurement
- Contact‑profiling

## 9. Mounting on Prusa MK4

- Attach the CR‑Touch to a custom bracket or existing probe mount.
- Ensure the sharp probe tip is the lowest point.
- Calibrate Z‑offset manually.
- Use slow Z‑approach speeds for scanning.

---

## 10. Safety Notes

- Do not crash the probe into the surface at high speed.
- Keep the tungsten tip away from fingers — extremely sharp.
- Avoid magnetic interference near the Hall sensor.
